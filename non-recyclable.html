Red Dustbin

The red dustbin is designated for non-recyclable waste, including medical waste that cannot be processed or recycled. This type of waste needs to be properly disposed of to prevent contamination and environmental harm.

What is Medical Waste?

Medical waste includes any waste generated from healthcare activities such as hospitals, clinics, and nursing homes. This waste is often hazardous and needs to be handled with care to avoid any health risks or environmental damage.

Categories of Medical Waste:

  • Infectious Waste: Contaminated with pathogens (e.g., used bandages, gloves).
  • Sharps Waste: Needles, blades, and other sharp objects.
  • Pharmaceutical Waste: Expired or unused medications.
  • Chemical Waste: Hazardous chemicals or reagents.
  • Radioactive Waste: Waste from radiological procedures.

Procedure Before Dumping:

Before dumping medical waste, it must be treated and safely packaged. Hospitals and nursing homes should follow these steps:

  • Segregate waste into appropriate categories.
  • Use color-coded bins for different types of medical waste.
  • Ensure waste is treated if necessary (e.g., autoclaving).
  • Seal and label containers securely.
  • Arrange for collection by a licensed waste disposal service.
